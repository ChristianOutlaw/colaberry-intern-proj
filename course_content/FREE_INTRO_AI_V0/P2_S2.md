# Exploring Data

## What you'll learn
- Describe the journey from raw data to actionable insight
- Understand how dashboards and visualizations support human decisions
- See clearly where analytics ends and AI begins

## Key ideas
- Raw data needs to be collected, cleaned, analyzed, and visualized before it's useful to anyone
- Dashboards surface patterns for human judgment — they don't make decisions themselves
- Analytics explains what happened; AI predicts what might happen next

## Lesson

### The Data-to-Insight Journey

Data on its own is like a pile of raw ingredients. Before it can feed a decision, it goes through a process:

1. **Collect** — gather observations (sales figures, website clicks, sensor readings, survey responses)
2. **Clean** — fix errors, handle missing values, standardize formats so comparisons are valid
3. **Analyze** — identify patterns, trends, and outliers worth paying attention to
4. **Visualize** — communicate findings clearly in charts, graphs, and dashboards
5. **Decide** — a human uses the insight to take action

Each step adds meaning. By the time a business leader looks at a dashboard, hundreds of transformation steps have already happened in the background.

### What Dashboards Actually Do

A dashboard is a visual summary of data designed to help humans make better decisions. Good dashboards:
- Show the **right metrics** for the specific decision at hand
- Make patterns visible at a glance without requiring technical knowledge
- Let users drill into the data behind the numbers when needed

**Example:** A sales dashboard might show revenue by region, month-over-month change, and the top-performing products. It helps a manager ask: *Why did sales drop in the Southeast last quarter?* The dashboard doesn't answer that question — it helps the right human ask and investigate it.

> **Dashboards surface patterns. Humans interpret them and decide what to do.**

### Analytics vs. AI — Where's the Line?

| | Analytics (BI) | AI / Machine Learning |
|---|---|---|
| **Core question** | What happened? Why? | What will happen next? What should we do? |
| **Method** | Aggregate, summarize, visualize | Train a model, generate predictions |
| **Output** | Reports, charts, dashboards | Forecasts, recommendations, automation |
| **Example** | "Sales dropped 8% last month" | "Sales will likely drop 12% next month unless we run a promotion" |

Analytics and AI aren't competing approaches — they're sequential layers. AI depends on the same clean, organized data that good analytics requires. You can't skip straight to AI.

## Common mistakes

**"BI dashboards and AI are basically the same thing."**

They're different layers in the same data pipeline. BI shows you what already happened; AI forecasts what might happen next. A dashboard tells you sales dropped — an AI model predicts which customers are most likely to stop buying before it happens. Both matter, and AI without a solid analytics foundation is far less reliable.
