# What Is AI?

## What you'll learn
- Recognize AI in apps and services you already use every day
- Understand what "intelligence" really means for a machine
- Know what this course covers — and what it intentionally skips

## Key ideas
- AI is pattern recognition, not consciousness or magic
- Machines learn from examples, not by thinking
- AI is already embedded in everyday life — it just works quietly

## Lesson

### AI Is Already Around You

You've used AI today. When Google Maps rerouted you around traffic, when Netflix suggested a show you hadn't heard of, when your bank flagged an unusual charge — that was AI working silently in the background.

AI doesn't live only in research labs or science fiction. It powers:
- **Navigation** — predicting traffic and suggesting faster routes in real time
- **Streaming** — matching your watch history against millions of users to surface recommendations
- **Email** — sorting spam before it reaches your inbox
- **Fraud detection** — flagging transactions that don't match your behavior patterns

None of these systems are magic. They're software trained on millions of examples until they became reliable at recognizing specific patterns.

### What "Intelligence" Actually Means in Machines

When we call a machine "intelligent," we don't mean it thinks, feels, or understands. We mean it can:

1. Take in data (recorded examples and inputs)
2. Find patterns across those examples
3. Make predictions or decisions based on those patterns

A spam filter doesn't read your email the way you do. It recognizes patterns — specific words, sender addresses, formatting quirks — that previous spam messages shared. That's the whole trick.

> **AI = pattern recognition + prediction. Not thinking. Not consciousness.**

### What This Course Will (and Won't) Do

This course is about understanding AI — not writing code for it. You won't touch a programming language. You will:
- Build a clear mental model of how AI systems actually work
- Learn to recognize AI in the world around you
- Understand why data is central to everything AI does

By the end, you'll be able to explain AI clearly to anyone — and you'll have a concrete sense of whether deeper technical learning is the right next step for you.

## Common mistakes

**"AI is magic — or too advanced for a non-technical person to understand."**

It's neither. AI is math, data, and logic — designed to find patterns in examples. If you've ever used a navigation app or gotten a streaming recommendation, you've been interacting with AI systems hundreds of times. The mystery goes away once you see what's happening underneath the interface.
